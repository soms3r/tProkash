# Contributor Guidelines

## Purpose

Define how community members can contribute data to the tProkash ecosystem. These guidelines ensure that all contributions meet quality standards and respect the integrity of the publishing data commons.

## Scope

All users with the Contributor role or higher (including Verified Contributors, Reviewers, Editors, and Administrators acting in a contributor capacity). This document covers data submission, source citation, conduct expectations, and the role upgrade path.

## Workflow / Process

### Getting Started

1. **Registration** — Create a tProkash account with a valid email address.
2. **Email Verification** — Verify your email to gain the Contributor role.
3. **Read the Guidelines** — Familiarize yourself with this document and the Submission Workflow.
4. **Start Contributing** — Submit your first records through the web form or bulk import.

### Role Upgrade Path

| Step | Requirement | Role Granted |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Email verified | Contributor |
| 2 | Phone or government ID verified + 25 accepted submissions with < 10% rejection rate | Verified Contributor |
| 3 | Invitation or application + demonstrated expertise | Reviewer |
| 4 | Appointment by Administrator based on track record | Editor |

### Data Submission Rules

- **Always cite a source.** Every submission must include at least one verifiable source. Preferred sources are official registries, publisher websites, ISBN databases, and library catalogs.
- **Never fabricate data.** Do not invent names, dates, or identifiers. If you are unsure about a data point, leave it blank.
- **Prefer official sources.** A publisher's own website is more reliable than a third-party listing. Government registries are preferred over commercial databases.
- **Bilingual names required.** For supported locales, provide both the English transliteration and the local-language name.
- **One record per submission.** Each submission form should contain one distinct entity. For batch submissions, use the bulk import feature.
- **No duplicate submissions.** Check whether the entity already exists before submitting (see Duplicate Detection).

### Quality Expectations

- **Accuracy**: Ensure all submitted data matches the cited source.
- **Completeness**: Fill in all fields you can reliably source. Leave blank any fields you cannot verify.
- **Source citation**: Provide stable, publicly accessible URLs or document uploads.
- **Timeliness**: Respond to change requests from Reviewers within 7 days.

## Decision Rules

### Prohibited Conduct

The following actions are prohibited and may result in immediate suspension or ban:

- Submitting fabricated or deliberately false data.
- Submitting copyrighted material without explicit license or permission.
- Uploading malicious files or scripts.
- Spamming — submitting the same record multiple times or flooding the system.
- Harassing Reviewers, Editors, or other Contributors.
- Attempting to manipulate or game the contributor reputation system.
- Creating multiple accounts to circumvent submission limits or moderation actions.

### Violation Consequences

| Violation | First Offense | Second Offense | Third Offense |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fabricated data | Warning + rejection | 30-day suspension | Permanent ban |
| Copyright violation | Content removal + warning | Account suspension | Permanent ban |
| Spam | Warning + rate limit reduction | 14-day suspension | Permanent ban |
| Harassment | Warning | 30-day suspension | Permanent ban |

## Examples

**Example 1: First submission by a new Contributor.** Jane registers, verifies her email, and submits a new publisher record for "O'Reilly Media." She fills in the legal name, website URL (oreilly.com), country (United States), and attaches the Wikipedia page as a source. The submission passes validation and enters the review queue with ID `SUB-2026-07-00051`.

**Example 2: Becoming a Verified Contributor.** After 25 accepted submissions with a 4% rejection rate, Jane is prompted to verify her phone number. She completes verification and is upgraded to Verified Contributor, gaining the ability to submit up to 50 records per day and have her submissions expedited in the review queue.

## Future Improvements

- Contributor reputation scores with badges for milestones (100, 500, 1000 accepted submissions).
- Gamification elements: leaderboards, monthly contribution challenges, and achievement badges.
- Direct messaging between Contributors and Reviewers for collaborative improvement.
- Contributor-curated collections and thematic data drives.
- Mentorship program where experienced Contributors guide newcomers.
