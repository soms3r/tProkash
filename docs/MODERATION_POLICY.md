# Moderation Policy

## Purpose

Define what content is permitted within the tProkash ecosystem, how policy violations are identified and handled, and the procedures for appeals and dispute resolution. This policy protects the integrity of the data commons and ensures a safe, respectful environment for all users.

## Scope

All user-generated content across the platform, including entity records, reviews, comments, user profiles, forum posts, and attached media files. This policy applies to all users regardless of role — from Guests to Administrators.

## Workflow / Process

### Permitted Content

Content is permitted if it:

- Is factual, accurate, and verifiable.
- Respects intellectual property rights (properly licensed or public domain).
- Respects privacy (no personal information without explicit consent).
- Is civil and respectful in tone.
- Serves the purpose of the platform: building a comprehensive, accurate publishing data commons.

### Prohibited Content

The following content types are strictly prohibited:

1. **Fabricated Data** — Any record or data point that is knowingly false or cannot be verified from any reliable source.
2. **Copyrighted Material** — Content used without proper license or permission, including cover images, excerpts, and full texts.
3. **Personal Information** — Private contact details, identification numbers, or other personal data submitted without the subject's explicit consent.
4. **Offensive Content** — Hate speech, slurs, threats, harassment, or any content that promotes discrimination or violence.
5. **Spam** — Duplicate content, promotional material unrelated to publishing data, or content designed to manipulate search rankings.
6. **Malicious Content** — Links to malware, phishing sites, or other harmful resources.

### Moderation Workflow

1. **Report** — Any user can report potentially prohibited content using the reporting tool. Reports require a reason and optional evidence.
2. **Triage** — A Moderator (Editor or Administrator) reviews the report within 24 hours for urgent cases, 72 hours for standard cases.
3. **Investigation** — The Moderator examines the content, user history, and any supporting evidence.
4. **Action** — The Moderator applies the appropriate moderation action (see below).
5. **Notification** — The content submitter and the reporter are notified of the outcome.
6. **Appeal** — The affected user may appeal the decision within 14 days.

## Decision Rules

### Moderation Actions

| Action | Applied For | Duration | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Warning | First-time or minor violations | Permanent record | No service restriction |
| Content Removal | Prohibited content (any) | Permanent | Content removed from public view |
| Temporary Suspension | Repeat or serious violations | 14–90 days | Account locked, no submissions |
| Permanent Ban | Severe or repeated violations | Permanent | Account terminated, no re-registration |

### Moderation Tiers

| Severity | Examples | Default Action |
|---|---|---|
| Low | Minor formatting issue, missing source on first submission | Warning |
| Medium | Unlicensed cover image, first copyright violation | Content removal + warning |
| High | Fabricated data, harassment, spam campaign | Temporary suspension (30 days) |
| Critical | Malicious links, hate speech, repeated severe violations | Permanent ban |

### Appeals Process

1. The affected user submits an appeal within 14 calendar days of the moderation action.
2. The appeal must include a justification and any supporting evidence.
3. An Administrator who did not issue the original action reviews the appeal.
4. The Administrator may: uphold the action, reduce the severity, or overturn the action entirely.
5. The appeal decision is final and communicated within 7 business days.

### Dispute Resolution

When two users disagree about the accuracy or appropriateness of content:

1. An Editor mediates the dispute, reviewing all submitted evidence and sources.
2. The Editor may request additional information from both parties.
3. The Editor issues a binding decision within 10 business days.
4. If the dispute involves a Moderation action, the Appeals process takes precedence.

## Examples

**Example 1: Fabricated data.** A user submits a publisher record with a fabricated legal name and a non-existent registration number. A Reviewer flags the submission during review. An Editor investigates, finds no supporting sources, and rejects the submission with a warning to the user. The user's account is noted for monitoring.

**Example 2: Offensive content in a review.** A user posts a review containing offensive language targeting an author. Another user reports the review. An Editor reviews, confirms the violation, removes the content, and issues a 30-day suspension to the offending user, noting a prior warning on the account.

## Future Improvements

- Automated content filtering using keyword and pattern detection for common violations.
- Community moderation program where trusted Verified Contributors can flag and triage low-severity reports.
- Appeal dashboard for users to track the status of their appeals in real time.
- Machine learning classification to prioritize high-severity reports for faster response.
- Transparent moderation log showing aggregate statistics on actions taken and appeals resolved.
